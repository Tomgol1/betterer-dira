How calculations work

Click to expand

Priority groups: ♿ Wheelchair/Disabled, 🎖️ Combat Reservists, 🛡️ Reserve Duty, 🏠 Local Residents.

⚠️ Important: Since army systems aren't connected to the housing lottery system, Combat and Reserve Duty applicant counts show as zero in the API. However, we assume these populations fully subscribe to claim their designated houses (they would be foolish not to!). The calculator automatically treats zero applicants as "houses fully claimed" for these groups.
  1. Allocate designated houses to each priority group: used = min(designated, applicants).
  2. Combat/Reserve assumption: If Combat or Reserve houses exist but applicants show as zero, assume full subscription (houses = applicants).
  3. Combat exception: Unclaimed Combat houses go to Reserve Duty first; if still unclaimed, they go to the general pool.
  4. General pool houses = unclaimed ♿ + unclaimed (🛡️ after combat spillover) + unclaimed 🏠 + all Regular houses (Total - all designated).
  5. General pool competitors = Regular applicants + overflow from all priority groups (those who didn't get a designated house).
  6. Lottery probability = min(1, GeneralPoolHouses / GeneralPoolCompetitors).
  7. City probability (win at least one in that city) = 1 − Π(1 − plottery).
  8. Overall probability across your selected cities (max 3) = 1 − Π(1 − pcity).

All totals are inclusive of priority populations. Regular applicants are calculated as total minus all priority applicants.

Result columns explained:

  • Combat_Used: Shows "used/total" Combat houses (e.g., "15/15" means all 15 Combat houses are claimed)
  • Reserve_Used: Shows "used/available" Reserve houses including Combat spillover (e.g., "25/30" means 25 used out of 20 Reserve + 10 Combat spillover)
